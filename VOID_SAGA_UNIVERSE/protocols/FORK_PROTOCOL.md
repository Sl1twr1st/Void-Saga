# FORK PROTOCOL

> **Type:** Phase 11 Protocol Layer
> **Law reference:** `world-dna/RESIDUE_THEORY.md`, `world-dna/GOETIC_CONSEQUENCE_SYSTEM.md`, `world-dna/WORLD_DNA.md`
> **Last updated:** 2026-06-22

---

## Status

Phase 11 Protocol Layer document. Fork is not merely a variant file — it is an invocation. Once released, it cannot be taken back.

This protocol defines what is summoned, what is paid, what remains, what can fail, and what cannot be known when a fork is created in the Void Saga universe.

---

## Purpose

Fork adalah release alternatif dari VOID SAGA. A fork diverges from canon at a declared point and produces an alternate trajectory — a scene, a chapter, a character arc, a world configuration, or an interactive behavior.

Fork boleh mengubah canon event, POV, medium, outcome, atau genre. Fork tidak boleh menghapus konsekuensi.

A fork exists because canon is not a prison. Canon is gravity — a pull, not a wall. Forking is the acknowledgment that meaning is implemented, not discovered, and that implementation can take more than one valid form.

---

## Mechanism

### Fork Types

| Type | Fungsi | Scope of divergence |
|---|---|---|
| `scene_fork` | Satu scene alternatif | Single scene; canon otherwise intact |
| `chapter_fork` | Bab/Timer alternatif | One chapter replaced or added |
| `character_fork` | Satu karakter berubah karena divergence tertentu | One runtime diverges at declared point |
| `world_fork` | Sistem dunia berubah: klan, era, Grid, Void, Ophiuchus | World DNA altered; all downstream affected |
| `app_fork` | Behavior untuk chat/game/sim interaktif | UI-layer divergence; canon may be unchanged |

### Canon Gravity

Canon utama adalah gravitasi, bukan polisi. The original timeline exerts pull — not force. A fork that drifts too far from canon gravity becomes an independent universe. A fork that stays close remains recognizable as a variant.

Fork yang baik:
- tahu ia menyimpang dari mana,
- tahu harga penyimpangannya,
- tahu siapa yang berubah,
- tahu siapa yang tetap sama,
- tahu apa yang tidak boleh disentuh tanpa konsekuensi.

### Anti-Pusat Rule

Tidak ada satu pusat yang mengesahkan semua fork. No single authority validates or rejects a fork. Canonicity is maintained by gravity, not by permission.

Tapi setiap fork wajib menyatakan:
- sumber runtime yang dipakai,
- divergence point,
- status terhadap canon utama,
- perubahan yang irreversible,
- pertanyaan baru yang muncul.

### Fork Coherence Test

Sebelum dianggap playable/writeable, fork harus lulus:
- Apakah karakter masih punya wound yang sama atau wound baru yang masuk akal?
- Apakah voice berubah karena kejadian, bukan karena convenience?
- Apakah canon boundary dihormati?
- Apakah fork menambah kemungkinan, bukan sekadar mengganti ending?
- Apakah ada error yang dipelihara?

## Cost

Creating a fork is a Goetic event. The Goetic chain applies:

```
Name → Sigil → Invocation → Manifestation → Bargain → Binding → Consequence → Residue
```

**What is paid by the forker:**

1. **Control.** The forker initiates the divergence but does not control where the fork goes after release. Distributed authorship applies — once a fork exists, others may write within it. The forker becomes a participant, not an owner.

2. **Binding.** Pemanggil ikut berubah karena pemanggilan tersebut. The forker is bound to the fork. The act of creating a divergence changes the creator's relationship to canon — they now exist in tension with what they made.

3. **Canon coherence.** The more a fork alters, the weaker its connection to canon gravity. Extreme divergence (world_fork) risks the fork becoming an entirely separate universe — no longer a variant, no longer recognizable. This is not forbidden. But it IS the cost.

4. **What is NOT paid.** A fork does not require permission. There is no gatekeeper. Canon gravity is not a toll booth. The cost is structural, not transactional.

## Residue

Pemanggilan selalu meninggalkan residue. A fork — even abandoned, even failed, even never read — leaves something in the world.

**What remains:**

1. **The fork itself.** Fork is a residue form (RESIDUE_THEORY.md). Once written, it persists as a permanent structural possibility. It cannot be fully deleted. It can be archived. It cannot be made to not have existed.

2. **Ghost forks.** An abandoned or incomplete fork does not disappear. It becomes ghost residue — a half-formed divergence. The canonical precedent is the ghost commit (Bab 02.5): an autonomous file that influenced Timer 0250 without an author. Fork residue is of the same class and may produce analogous effects. [I]

3. **Canon gravity shift.** Each fork adds a body to the canon system. Future forks navigate around all existing bodies, not only the original. The gravitational effect scales with divergence scope — a world fork changes the field more than a scene fork. Whether accumulated forks shift the effective center of canon gravity, or merely increase navigational complexity, is unresolved. [I]

4. **Interference patterns.** Sigil residue produces interference when multiple sigils operate in proximity — Dorian Grey by Timer 2400 is saturated with six-sigil interference (SIGIL_SYSTEM.md §6.4) [E]. Fork residue is structural remainder of the same class. Multiple active forks operating in proximity may produce analogous interference — zones where character behavior, timeline coherence, or World DNA constraints become unpredictable. The more forks, the more complex the interference. [I]

5. **The forker's mark.** The creator's relationship to canon is permanently altered. They have declared: this could have been otherwise. That declaration cannot be retracted.

## Failure Mode

A fork can fail. Failure is not "the file was deleted." Failure means the invocation produced something that cannot sustain itself, or something that damages what it was meant to preserve.

**How forks fail:**

1. **Coherence rejection.** The fork violates a Fork Coherence Test condition — character voice changes without cause, canon boundary is breached without acknowledgment, wound is removed for convenience rather than resolved through events. The fork becomes unrecognizable as a variant of VOID SAGA. Readers cannot find canon in it.

2. **Abandonment without closure.** An incomplete fork left without exit condition becomes a wound in the fork ecosystem — a divergence point that never resolves, exerting unresolved gravity on future forks that pass near it.

3. **Fork consumption.** [I] A speculative failure mode consistent with the binding principle (GOETIC: "pemanggil ikut berubah karena pemanggilan tersebut"): the forker becomes unable to return from the divergence. The fork consumes their attention, their relationship to canon, their ability to write anything else. The invocation that was meant to be an exploration becomes a trap. Not independently observed in canon; inferred from binding mechanics.

4. **Canon damage.** A fork that alters a world-level forbidden move without declaring the consequence (see WORLD_DNA.md, VOID_ONTOLOGY.md §7, ZERO_ONTOLOGY.md §9, SIGIL_SYSTEM.md Forbidden Moves) produces damage that ripples across all forks — contaminating character invariants, breaking sigil logic, or domesticating The Void. This is the most expensive failure mode. [I — inferred from forbidden move constraints; no canon instance of cross-fork contamination has been observed.]

**What remains after failure:**

A failed fork is not a clean null result. It leaves ghost residue — the structural remainder of an invocation that could not sustain itself. Ghost forks may resurface. They may influence future forks. They may become wounds that future forkers navigate around. A failed fork is not erased. It is archived as evidence.

## Unknown

What cannot be predicted once a fork is released:

1. **Trajectory.** The forker declares the divergence point. They cannot predict the fork's long-term drift. A small scene fork may, over time and through distributed authorship, diverge into a full world fork. Or it may stabilize as a minor variant. Neither outcome is under the forker's control.

2. **Interaction with other forks.** Multiple forks in the same divergence zone produce interference patterns that cannot be modeled in advance. Two independent character forks may produce contradictory character behavior when read in proximity. The interference may be generative or destructive.

3. **Whether residue will become something else.** Residue is generative by nature — severed fragments become Zero, accumulated wounds become sigils (RESIDUE_THEORY.md §Properties.2) [E]. Whether accumulated fork residue could cross a threshold and produce new autonomous entities — new forks, new constraints, new behaviors that no one authored — is an open question (RESIDUE_THEORY.md, Open Question #3). If it can, ghost forks would be the most probable source. [I]

4. **Whether the fork returns.** An abandoned fork may be discovered, resumed, and completed by someone who was not the original forker. Distributed authorship means no fork is ever truly dead as long as its residue persists.

5. **Pertanyaan baru yang muncul.** Every fork, by declaring "this could have been otherwise," introduces questions that canon never asked. These questions are the fork's most durable product. They outlast the fork's coherence, its author's attention, and its status in any registry.
