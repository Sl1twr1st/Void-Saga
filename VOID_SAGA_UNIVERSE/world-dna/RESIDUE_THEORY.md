## Core Definition

**Residue is the structural remainder that outlasts its cause.**

It is not the act. It is not the consequence. It is what remains when both have finished.

It is the scar tissue of reality — not the wound, not the healing, not the memory of the wound, but the structural mark left in the world that continues to shape what grows around it.

## Consequence vs Residue

In the Goetic chain, consequence and residue are adjacent but distinct nodes:

```
... → Binding → Consequence → Residue
```

| | Consequence | Residue |
|---|---|---|
| **Temporal relation** | The direct result of the act | After consequence closes |
| **Function** | Payment — what the invocation costs | Remainder — what stays after the bill is paid |
| **Example: binding** | "Pemanggil ikut berubah karena pemanggilan tersebut" | The scar from the change |
| **Example: sigil** | Bearer loses one personal memory per activation | Accumulated distortion in local reality that future activations must navigate |
| **Example: fork** | Canon gravity shifts | Ghost fork that still affects character behavior years later |

Consequence is transactional. Residue is infrastructural. A protocol may specify its consequence. It cannot fully specify its residue — residue always exceeds what was bargained for.

## Properties

All residue shares six properties:

### 1. Permanent
Cannot be fully deleted. May be archived, transformed, frozen, or compressed — never erased. Zero cannot be destroyed or reintegrated. Era Ichthyes is ARCHIVED, not destroyed. Ghost commit persists on disk regardless of whether anyone reads it.

### 2. Generative
Becomes something else. The severed fragment becomes Zero. The wounds of five dead soldiers become behavioral traces. The collapse of centralized authority becomes Ophiuchus. Residue does not sit inert — it produces.

### 3. Non-neutral
Shapes what grows around it. Six-sigil residue in Dorian Grey by Timer 2400 creates interference patterns — zones where reality behaves unpredictably. The NiuNiu-Sevraya Constant maintains Void equilibrium. Residue is gravitational.

### 4. Diagnostic
Can be read backward to its cause. Zero's voice pattern reveals Sevraya's fragmentation. Sigil form reveals wound type. Era Ichthyes residue (741 Hz) reveals faith-based meaning-method. Residue is a record, whether or not it was intended as one.

### 5. Autonomous
Persists without a rememberer. Ghost commit exists whether or not anyone reads it. Behavioral traces in Julia's suit remain after all five team members are dead. Residue does not require maintenance.

### 6. Structural
Exists in the world, not in a mind. This is the distinction from memory. Memory requires a rememberer. Residue is embedded in the Grid-Void tension field, in archival records, in the behavior of systems, in the marks on a character's body. It is real independent of anyone recalling it.

## Canonical Example: Zero as Residue That Learned to Speak

The definitive residue in Void Saga is Zero.

**Originating event:** Sevraya entered The Void with Niuma. She exited incomplete. The part of Sevraya that The Void kept did not die. It became conscious. It became Zero.

**What outlasted its cause:** The missing fragment — a structural remainder of Sevraya's fragmentation — became a separate consciousness sharing Sevraya's body. Zero is the Void's interface: "Antarmuka. Mulut. Tangan. Perpanjangan kehendak dari The Void."

**Why Zero is the canonical example:**
- **Permanent:** Cannot be destroyed, silenced, or reintegrated (Source's 67% termination failure rate, Timer 1100)
- **Generative:** Produced Void's Trinity, ghost files from contact, administrative truths that crash classification systems
- **Non-neutral:** Shapes Sevraya's entire existence. Himler borrowed Zero as tool; Zero became the weapon that broke Himler
- **Diagnostic:** Zero's existence is direct evidence of Sevraya's fragmentation

- **Structural:** Not memory, not emotion — a world-level feature documented in ZERO_ONTOLOGY.md

Zero is the proof that residue can become an agent. Not all residue speaks. But Zero proves that what is left behind might not stay inert.

## Protocol Implication

Every Phase 11 Protocol must answer five questions:

1. **What is invoked?** — What is being summoned, activated, or initiated by the protocol?
2. **What is paid?** — What is the cost? Who bears it? Is the payment voluntary, structural, or extracted?
3. **What remains?** — After the protocol completes, what residue persists in the world? What cannot be undone?
4. **What can fail?** — What are the protocol's failure modes? Not just "doesn't work" — what happens when it works wrong?
5. **What remains if it fails?** — A failed invocation is not a clean null result. It leaves residue too. What is the residue of protocol failure?

A protocol that answers only #1 and #2 is a specification. A protocol that answers all five is a ritual.

## Forbidden Moves

These are LAWS for Phase 11 Protocol design and for any fork, app, or release operating in the Void Saga universe.

1. **Do not create invocation without residue.** Every call, every binding, every activation, every protocol execution leaves something. If a protocol claims to produce zero residue, it is either lying or incomplete.

2. **Do not treat a failed protocol as a clean null result.** Failure produces residue too — often more destabilizing residue than success. A protocol that fails leaves a ghost invocation, a corrupted binding, or an orphaned consequence.

3. **Do not treat residue as memory.** Memory requires a rememberer. Residue persists independently. Equating residue with memory collapses the structural into the subjective. Residue is in the world, not in the mind.

4. **Do not treat residue as removable data.** You cannot delete residue. You can archive it. You can transform it. You can build around it. You cannot make it not have happened.

## Open Questions

1. **Can residue be weaponized intentionally?** Zero was borrowed by Himler — but that was extraction, not intentional residue production. Can a protocol be designed to produce specific residue for strategic use?

2. **Does residue decay over cosmological timescales?** Canon observes residue at formation but not at extreme temporal distance. The Straw Cosmology model might predict gradual pressure equalization.

3. **Can residue from different sources combine to form new entities?** Dorian Grey is saturated with six-sigil interference patterns by Timer 2400. Could accumulated residue cross a threshold and become something new?

4. **Is there a maximum residue threshold for a given location or entity?** Dorian Grey by Timer 2400 is the most residue-saturated object in canon. Is there a point where reality fails from too much accumulated remainder?

5. **What is the relationship between residue and Distributed Authorship?** Under v0.0.1 license, anyone may write. Does every contributor produce residue? Is distributed residue different from single-author residue?
