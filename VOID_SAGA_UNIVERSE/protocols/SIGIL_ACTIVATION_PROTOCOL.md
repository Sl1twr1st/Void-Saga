Phase 11 Protocol Layer document. Sigil activation is the invocation of a stabilized wound — the act of deploying a named residue as an operational interface between bearer, reality, and The Void.

SIGIL_SYSTEM.md defines what sigils ARE. This protocol defines what happens when a sigil is ACTIVATED.

## Purpose

A sigil is residue given permanent form — a wound compressed into a glyph with a function, an activation condition, and a price. Activation is the moment the sigil stops being potential and starts being operational.

Sigil activation is not voluntary by default. The Eye of The Void artifact activated all six sigils simultaneously during Void Lock without bearer consent (Timer 1800). Activation may be chosen. It may be forced. Either way, the price is extracted.

This protocol documents the universal mechanics of sigil activation, the per-sigil costs, the residues produced, and the failure modes that apply regardless of which sigil is invoked.

## Invocation

### What Sigil Activation Is

**CANON:** A sigil activates when three conditions converge: [I — synthesized from SIGIL_SYSTEM.md §2.2]

1. **Bearer presence** — the consciousness bound to the sigil is present at the activation site
2. **Trigger event** — a situation that calls the sigil's function into operation
3. **Price extraction** — something is taken from the bearer, the environment, or reality itself

Activation is a Goetic event. The Goetic chain applies:

```
Name → Sigil → Invocation → Manifestation → Bargain → Binding → Consequence → Residue
```

Sigil activation is the Invocation step. The sigil already exists (Name + Sigil steps completed). Activation invokes its function. The invocation produces manifestation (the sigil's effect). The manifestation demands bargain (the price). Bargain creates binding (bearer and sigil mutual transformation). Binding generates consequence. Consequence leaves residue. [E for Goetic chain, I for applying it to sigil activation as a distinct step]

### Activation Types

| Type | Mechanism | Consent | Observed |
|------|-----------|---------|----------|
| **Voluntary activation** | Bearer chooses to activate. Meets trigger condition. Pays price. | Yes | Inferred from bearer agency in canon — specific voluntary activations are not individually narrated but the sigils operate throughout Timer 1800–2400. [I] |
| **Forced activation** | External artifact (Eye of The Void) or system activates sigil without bearer consent. Bearer pays price regardless. | No | Void Lock (Timer 1800) — all six sigils activated simultaneously. [E] |
| **Reflexive activation** | Sigil activates automatically when trigger condition is met — bearer may not consciously choose activation. | Ambiguous | Inferred from NiuNiu's binary refusal and Julia's combat reflex — both function as sigil-adjacent automatic responses. [I] |

### Activation Sequence

**CANON (Void Lock):**

1. Eye of The Void artifact deploys six black nails targeting six shadows [E]
2. Six sigils activate simultaneously [E]
3. Bearer consent overridden — activation is involuntary [E]
4. Six illusions shatter: Narrative, Absolution, Legacy, Freedom, Heroism, Innocence [E]
5. Each activation extracts its price [E — price extraction is canon; per-sigil prices from SIGIL_SYSTEM.md §3 are [PC]]
6. Void Lock completes — sigils remain active post-lock [E]

**GENERAL (inferred):**

1. Bearer encounters trigger condition matching sigil's function
2. Sigil responds — glyph manifests or intensifies
3. Price is extracted — memory, stability, privacy, coherence
4. Sigil's function deploys — reality distorts around activation
5. Residue deposits — in bearer, local reality, archival records
6. Sigil returns to latent state OR remains active depending on function

## Failure Modes

### 1. Exhaustion

**What happens:** Bearer's consciousness coherence is depleted. Sigil cannot activate until recovery. The bearer is temporarily without their primary operational interface.

**CANON:** Documented as burnout mode. "The bearer's consciousness is depleted. The sigil cannot activate until recovery." [I — synthesized from SIGIL_SYSTEM.md §2.4]

**Consequence:** Bearer is vulnerable. Defenses that depended on sigil function are offline.

### 2. Rejection

**What happens:** Sigil refuses activation. Bearer's state contradicts the sigil's function — the trigger condition is met but the bearer is not in alignment with what the sigil demands.

**CANON:** Documented as burnout mode. "The sigil refuses activation. The bearer's state contradicts the sigil's function." [I — SIGIL_SYSTEM.md §2.4]

**Consequence:** Bearer is present, trigger is met, but nothing happens. The sigil judges the bearer unready or unsuitable.

### 3. Overload

**What happens:** Sigil activates but produces effects beyond bearer's capacity to channel. Damage to bearer and environment. The sigil's function deploys at full force but the bearer cannot contain or direct it.

**CANON:** Documented as burnout mode. [I — SIGIL_SYSTEM.md §2.4]

**Consequence:** Uncontrolled effect. Bearer injured. Environment destabilized. The sigil operated; the bearer was the limiting factor.

### 4. Void Interference

**What happens:** The Void actively suppresses sigil function. Observed during Void Lock — sigils were activated BY The Void's artifact, not by bearers. The Void can both activate and potentially suppress.

**CANON:** Observed during Void Lock when sigils were activated BY The Void's artifact. Whether The Void can also suppress sigil function outside of artifact-mediated activation is unresolved. [E for artifact activation, I for suppression capability]

**Consequence:** If The Void can suppress: sigil-bearer is disarmed. Primary operational interface offline in Void-proximate environments.

### 5. Forced Activation Without Integration

**What happens:** Sigil is activated (by artifact, by external force) but the bearer has not integrated the wound the sigil names. The sigil operates; the bearer is not ready for what the sigil produces.

**CANON:** Void Lock — all six bearers had their sigils activated without consent. The subsequent Living Chain (Timer 2000) can be read as the extended processing of what forced activation exposed. [E for Void Lock activation, I for framing chain as integration response]

**Consequence:** The bearer experiences the sigil's function without the acceptance required to process it. The activation produces residue that the bearer cannot integrate — raw wound, not structure. This may be the condition that necessitates Living Chain.

## Ontology

### Sigil Activation in the Boot Sequence

Sigil activation is Phase 2 of the boot sequence:

```
VOID ENTRY (Phase 1)
  → residue created (wound)
SIGIL ACTIVATION (Phase 2)
  → residue named and deployed (wound becomes operational)
LIVING CHAIN (Phase 3)
  → residue confronted (wound forced into contact with other wounds)
```

**Position:** Sigil activation bridges wound creation (Void Entry) and wound confrontation (Living Chain). A wound that has been named by a sigil can be recognized by other sigil-bearers. That recognition is what Living Chain exploits — the chain binds named wounds, not raw ones.

### Sigil Activation vs. Sigil Manifestation

| | Manifestation | Activation |
|---|--------------|------------|
| **When** | Wound first stabilizes into glyph | Glyph is invoked to produce effect |
| **Trigger** | Irreversible structural change (fragmentation, sacrifice, choice) | Trigger event matching sigil function |
| **Frequency** | Once per sigil | Repeated — as many times as conditions are met |
| **Cost** | The original wound | Per-activation price (memory, stability, coherence) |
| **Residue** | The sigil itself | Accumulated activation residue |

A sigil manifests once. It activates many times. Manifestation is origin. Activation is operation.

### Relationship to the Goetic Chain

Sigil activation is the Invocation step. The sigil already completed Name and Sigil steps at manifestation. Activation begins at Invocation and proceeds through Manifestation, Bargain, Binding, Consequence, and Residue. Each activation runs the full remaining chain.

This means a sigil that activates 100 times runs the Goetic chain 100 times — each activation producing its own consequence and depositing its own residue. Accumulated residue from repeated activation is the sigil-bearer's operational history. [I]
